============================================================
Column definitions
============================================================

  Clean Full F1         Macro-F1 on full clean UNSW test set.
                         - Baseline/S2: standard Macro-F1.
                         - Strategy 1: Macro-F1 treating REJECT as an error class.
                           This is intentionally low (0.1157) because rejecting 5.4% of
                           samples counts all of them as wrong.

  Coverage              Fraction of test samples accepted (not rejected).
                         - Strategy 1 only; N/A for methods without rejection.

  Accepted F1           Macro-F1 on accepted (non-rejected) samples only.
                         - This is a selective metric: it measures performance on the
                           subset the strategy chooses to answer, NOT full-set robustness.

  Stress A F1           Known-class Macro-F1 under open-set conditions (held-out unknown
                         classes). For Strategy 1 this is accepted-only F1 after rejection
                         (selective metric). For Strategy 2 this is majority-vote F1.

  Stress B F1           Binary F1 on CICIDS2018 cross-dataset evaluation.
                         "0% cov / undef." means zero coverage: all CICIDS samples were
                         rejected at that tau, making accepted-sample metrics undefined.

  Stress C F1           Macro-F1 under Gaussian noise (sigma=0.1).
                         "0% cov / undef." means rejection removed all noisy samples.

============================================================
Key Interpretive Warnings
============================================================

1. Strategy 1 metrics are SELECTIVE (accepted-only) unless labeled "Full F1".
   Accepted F1 and Stress A F1 for Strategy 1 measure performance on the subset
   the model chooses to answer, not full-set robustness. Compare with Coverage
   and Full F1 to see the complete picture.

2. Stress B "0% cov / undef." is a FAILURE MODE, not "not tested".
   Strategy 1 at tau=0.99 rejects ALL target-domain samples (coverage=0.0000),
   resulting in undefined accepted-sample metrics. This is a failure mode under
   distribution shift: the confidence threshold learned on UNSW does not transfer.
   Strategy 2 majority vote F1 (0.2588) is nearly identical to baseline (0.2593).
   Neither strategy solves cross-dataset shift.

3. Primary metric: Macro-F1 (not Weighted-F1).
   With 94.6% Benign, Weighted-F1 is dominated by the majority class.
